Lake Powell is a place unlike any you've probably visited. By definition it's Glen Canyon, originally formed by the Colorado River. The reservior is created by a large dam only second in size to the Hoover Dam just downstream at Lake Mead (between the two dams is the Grand Canyon). While there's plenty of controversy around the lake, I can say it's a water playground unlike many others.

In short, you can take boats up and down a canyon nearly 150 miles long, with hundreds of gorges along the way. Some are long and wide, some are tall and narrow, and some end gradually up a slope where you can still find the trees submerged from decades ago.

It's not a busy place, you see maybe a half-dozen other boats each day (away from the marinas) and can easily keep to yourself, explore, and have a good time. When you park a houseboat there's plenty of room for swimming, hiking, even a campfire on the beaches.

One of the highlights is the Rainbow Bridge National Monument, one of the largest bridges in the world. It has a huge significance to the native Americans in the area and, respectfully, you keep your distance and do not walk up to (especially under) the bridge.

The Lake is a great place to sightsee, fish, swim, but even better for relaxing, sunbathing and catching up on reading. I caught up on 4 books and all my unread Instapaper articles while on vacation and had a great time eating, drinking, and enjoying family time.

I don't know if the photos do any justice, but it's an an amazing place to visit.
